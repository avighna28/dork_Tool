TECHNICAL BRIEFING

What is EXIF?

EXIF (Exchangeable Image File Format) is a hidden layer of metadata embedded in photos. It acts as a digital fingerprint, recording device info, settings, and location.

The GPS Threat

If you upload a raw photo from your phone to a blog or forum, you might be broadcasting your exact GPS coordinates (Home, Work, School). Most major social networks strip this, but email and file sharing do not.

Defensive OPSEC

  • Always scrub metadata before sharing publicly.
  • Turn off "Location Tags" in your camera app.
  • Use tools like this to verify your files.
